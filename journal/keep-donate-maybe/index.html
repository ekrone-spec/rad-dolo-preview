Closet edit · 2 min read

Keep, donate, maybe

Every closet edit I do starts the same way: everything comes out, and we build three piles. Keep. Donate. Maybe.

Keep is the easy one. It's what you reach for without thinking — the jeans you'd grab if the house were on fire.

Donate is where people freeze. So I use one rule to break the tie: if it's ill-fitting, if it "sort of fits," or if the only reason it's still hanging there is I might need this someday — it goes.

That's the whole rule. "Sort of fits" is a no. "Someday" is a no. You are not a warehouse for a version of yourself who hasn't shown up in four years.

The maybe pile does the real work

Maybe is what makes the whole thing function. Anything you can't decide on goes there, and we don't argue about it — we move on. At the end, we come back and try to build an actual outfit out of each maybe. If it works with things you already own, it stays. If we can't style it, you have your answer, and you got there yourself instead of me telling you.

Here's what nobody warns you about: the hard part isn't the clothes, it's the story attached to them. The dress from the wedding. The jacket that cost too much to admit defeat on. The pants from before.

I'm not attached to any of it — and honestly, that's most of what you're paying me for. An outside eye with no memory of what that sweater meant in 2019.

What it actually looks like

My clients routinely donate six to eight bags. One client and I filled eight. Nobody has ever called me later to say they miss something.

If you're doing this alone this weekend: pull everything out, don't sit down, and set a timer. The longer a piece sits in your hands, the more likely it ends up back on the rack.

Three piles. One rule. Start.

Your disheveled closet is my happy place.
